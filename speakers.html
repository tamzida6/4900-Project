Guest Speakers

Shahana Hanif is a Bangladeshi-American activist, organizer, and politician. She gained prominence as a community organizer and advocate for marginalized communities, particularly in Brooklyn, New York. Hanif has been involved in various social justice initiatives, including advocating for affordable housing, immigrant rights, and gender equality. In 2021, she made history by becoming the first South Asian and Muslim woman elected to the New York City Council, representing the 39th district. Throughout her career, Hanif has been a vocal advocate for inclusivity, representation, and progressive policies.

Tasneem Atik Sabri is a co-founder of Vela Hijabs, specializing in creating hijabs for Muslim women and beyond. She is particularly active in addressing issues related to Islamophobia, women's rights, and youth empowerment. Sabri has spoken at numerous events and conferences, sharing her insights on topics such as religious tolerance, diversity, and civic engagement. Rana Abdelhamid bis a well-known activist, organizer, and social entrepreneur. She is recognized for her advocacy work in empowering Muslim women and combating gender-based violence. Abdelhamid is the founder of the International Muslim Women's Initiative for Self-Empowerment (WISE), an organization dedicated to providing self-defense training and leadership development programs for Muslim women. She has also been involved in initiatives focused on community organizing, youth empowerment, and interfaith dialogue. Additionally, Abdelhamid has been recognized for her efforts in promoting social justice and equality through various speaking engagements, workshops, and advocacy campaigns.
