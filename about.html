About Us

Iqura Naheed

Naheed, a native of Staten Island, began her undergraduate journey at Brooklyn College in 2018 as a Macaulay Honors program student. Intrigued by the college's diversity, she was drawn to engage with various communities. While initially undecided about her major, Naheed always harbored a passion for medicine. After taking anthropology courses, she found her calling in studying human beings and the systems shaping our experiences, leading her to pursue anthropology with a minor in chemistry on the pre-medical track.

“I learned a lot as press director in terms of what student government is needed for,” she said, noting that many students checked USG’s announcements and socials for college-related updates. During her sophomore year, Naheed joined the student government as a senator, serving on the Student Affairs subcommittee. Recognizing the importance of student voices, especially during the pandemic, she worked tirelessly to advocate for her peers. As co-president, she collaborated with her partner to facilitate the transition back to in-person learning, addressing student concerns directly with administrators. Despite facing challenges as a woman of color and a Muslim woman in leadership, Naheed remained committed to representing her community and advocating for their needs.  

“Being a woman of color and especially a Muslim woman on the Brooklyn College campus, holding the position I hold, was a complicated sort of experience. It required me to be very particular of everything that I do, every event that I choose to show up to, and every position that I hold or things that I say that I feel like probably wouldn’t have been the case if I had any other identity,” In her role as co-president, Naheed prioritized ensuring that Muslim student concerns were heard and addressed by the college administration, given the community's history of being surveilled by an undercover NYPD officer on campus. Despite the complexities of her identity and the challenges it posed, Naheed persevered in her leadership role, mindful of the importance of every decision and action she took. She will continue her advocacy work as the program coordinator for the Muslim Women's Research Development Project at The Women's Center.
